# REFRAME Carousel Template

**Best for:** Challenging dominant assumptions in coaching, learning, or creation.
**Slide count:** 9 slides
**Hook pattern:** `You're not [X]. You're [Y].` — the provocation.

---

## Slide Arc

### Slide 1 — Provocation
**Role:** The hook. State the reframe before earning it. This is the intellectual sucker punch.
**Headline layer (`slide-1-headline`):** `You're not [X]. You're [Y].`
**Headline-accent layer (`slide-1-headline-accent`):** The key contrasting word (Y) — DM Sans Bold, gold `#C8A96E`
**Body layer (`slide-1-body`):** Leave empty. The headline is the entire slide.
**Label layer (`slide-1-label`):** `ATTUNE` (Geist Mono, tracked caps, gold)
**Tone:** Provocative. Confident. No hedging. The reader should feel slightly called out.

**Copy prompt:** Identify the thing people think they're doing (X) and what they're actually doing (Y). X should be aspirational or positive. Y should be the uncomfortable truth. Examples:
- "You're not building skill. You're building compliance."
- "You're not training athletes. You're training robots."
- "You're not writing content. You're performing productivity."
- "You're not coaching movement. You're prescribing programs."

---

### Slide 2 — Setup
**Role:** Pause. Acknowledge the reader's confusion. Then promise the distinction.
**Headline layer (`slide-2-headline`):** `Here's the difference:`
**Body layer (`slide-2-body`):** 1-2 sentences bridging the provocation to the explanation. Do not explain yet — build anticipation.
**Tone:** Patient. Slightly conspiratorial. "Lean in."

**Copy prompt:** Write a 1-2 sentence bridge that acknowledges most people conflate X and Y, then signals that the distinction is real and consequential. Example: "Most coaches use these words interchangeably. The difference isn't semantic. It determines whether skill transfers when it matters most."

---

### Slide 3 — Wrong Version
**Role:** Concrete illustration of X (the wrong version). Specific. Recognizable.
**Headline layer (`slide-3-headline`):** `[X] looks like this:`
**Body layer (`slide-3-body`):** 3-4 sentences. A specific scenario or behavior that exemplifies the wrong version. The reader should recognize this from their own experience or observation.
**Label layer (`slide-3-label`):** `THE OLD WAY` (Geist Mono, small, gold)
**Tone:** Precise. Not mocking. The reader has probably done this.

**Copy prompt:** Paint a scene of X in action. Specific coach, athlete, creator, or learner behavior. Make it recognizable without being a caricature. The goal is honest recognition, not ridicule.

---

### Slide 4 — Right Version (with comparison grid)
**Role:** Concrete illustration of Y (the correct version). Use the 2-column comparison grid layout for maximum contrast.
**Headline layer (`slide-4-headline`):** `[Y] looks like this:`
**Col-left-header layer (`slide-4-col-left-header`):** Left column header (e.g. `THE OLD WAY` or `X`)
**Col-right-header layer (`slide-4-col-right-header`):** Right column header (e.g. `THE ECOLOGICAL WAY` or `Y`)
**Col-left-body layer (`slide-4-col-left-body`):** Left column content — key stats or descriptors of the wrong version
**Col-right-body layer (`slide-4-col-right-body`):** Right column content — key stats or descriptors of the right version
**Accent layer (`slide-4-accent`):** Optional pull quote: the clearest one-line articulation of the contrast.
**Tone:** Generative. The reader should feel possibility, not shame.

**Copy prompt:** Write the comparison as parallel contrasting pairs. Use the BJJMM grid format: bold large numbers or descriptors in the left column (wrong version) vs. right column (right version). Example from BJJMM: Wrestling/Soccer: 50-100+ competitive reps/year vs. BJJ Tournaments: 3-6 competitive reps/year.

---

### Slide 5 — Research Anchor
**Role:** Name the principle. Give it a research home. This is the credential slide.
**Headline layer (`slide-5-headline`):** `The research calls this [concept].`
**Body layer (`slide-5-body`):** 2-3 sentences defining the concept and its source. Name the thinker or study.
**Label layer (`slide-5-label`):** Thinker name (e.g., `GIBSON` / `BERNSTEIN` / `DAVIDS` / `GRAY`) — Geist Mono, gold
**Tone:** Authoritative. Grounded. Not academic — translated.

**Copy prompt:** Name the ecological dynamics principle that explains the Y version. Cite Bernstein, Gibson, Davids, Newell, Gray, or Araújo by name. Translate the technical definition into Sam's plain language. Example: "The research calls this affordance-based learning. Gibson showed that skill is not stored in the body — it lives in the relationship between the organism and the information structure of the environment."

---

### Slide 6 — Domain Example 1
**Role:** Sport or coaching application. First cross-domain proof.
**Headline layer (`slide-6-headline`):** `Example from coaching:`
**Body layer (`slide-6-body`):** 3-4 sentences. A specific drill, practice design, or coaching decision that demonstrates the Y version in a sport context.
**Tone:** Concrete. Scene-based. The reader can visualize this in a training session.

**Copy prompt:** Write a specific coaching or sport scenario that shows the Y version (the ecological approach) in action. Name a sport if it makes the example clearer. Draw from discus, team sports, motor learning research, or any domain Sam knows well.

---

### Slide 7 — Domain Example 2
**Role:** Second domain — creation, business, parenting, or writing. Expands the principle's range.
**Headline layer (`slide-7-headline`):** `Example from [creation / parenting / writing]:`
**Body layer (`slide-7-body`):** 3-4 sentences. Same principle applied to a non-sport context.
**Tone:** Resonant. The reader who isn't a coach should feel this.

**Copy prompt:** Take the same principle from slide 5 and apply it to a completely different domain: a writer designing their creative environment, a parent structuring how their child explores, a creator choosing which constraints to impose on their content practice. Cross the domain fully — don't just gesture at it.

---

### Slide 8 — Philosophical Close
**Role:** The reframe lands. Elevate from practical to philosophical. End the argument.
**Headline layer (`slide-8-headline`):** `The shift isn't technique. It's perception.`
**Body layer (`slide-8-body`):** 2-3 sentences. The deeper implication. What changes when you make this shift — not in what you do, but in how you see.
**Tone:** Elevated. Still. This is the Sam voice at its most philosophical.

**Copy prompt:** Write the philosophical close. What does the shift from X to Y ultimately require? It's not a new drill or a new framework — it's a change in what you notice, what you prioritize, what you consider real. This should feel like the last line of an essay that reframes everything that came before it.

---

### Slide 9 — CTA
**Role:** Action. Simple. Direct. One ask.
**Headline layer (`slide-9-headline`):** `Follow for more. Or grab the free Foundations intro.`
**Body layer (`slide-9-body`):** Optional: 1-line context. "Signal/Noise lands weekly. Link in bio."
**Label layer (`slide-9-label`):** `@attunemastery` (Geist Mono, gold)
**Tone:** Inviting. Not desperate. You've already given them value — the CTA is an afterthought done well.

**Copy prompt:** Write one follow CTA and one conversion CTA. Keep them short. The follow ask is for algorithmic reach. The conversion ask points to Signal/Noise or Foundations. Never summarize the carousel in the CTA slide.

---

## Layout Modules Used in REFRAME

**Slide 1:** Full-bleed headline only. Maximum visual weight. One gold impact word.
**Slides 2-3:** Headline + body. Label on slide 3.
**Slide 4:** Headline + 2-column comparison grid (BJJMM style). Left/right headers in contrasting colors.
**Slide 5:** Headline + body + label (thinker name). Credential slide.
**Slides 6-7:** Headline + body. Storytelling slides. Different domains.
**Slide 8:** Headline + body. Philosophical weight. Minimal layout.
**Slide 9:** Headline + optional 1-line body. Label = @attunemastery.

---

## Voice Checklist (run before finalizing all slides)

- [ ] First person (I) throughout
- [ ] No em dashes — colon or period
- [ ] No banned words: delve, realm, harness, unlock, tapestry, leverage, synergy, seamless, optimize
- [ ] No AI transitions: "Furthermore", "In conclusion", "It's worth noting", "Let's dive in"
- [ ] Slide 3 and Slide 4 use parallel structure for maximum contrast
- [ ] At least two domains crossed
- [ ] Slide 1 stands alone — no body text needed
- [ ] Slide 8 is philosophical — not practical, not a summary
- [ ] Slide 9 ends with invitation, not command
