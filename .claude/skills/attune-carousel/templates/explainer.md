# EXPLAINER Carousel Template

**Best for:** Named concepts from ecological dynamics that need unpacking.
**Slide count:** 8 slides
**Hook pattern:** `[Concept Name].` — full stop, bold, centered. Authority through stillness.

---

## Slide Arc

### Slide 1 — Hook
**Role:** Authority statement. The concept named and nothing else.
**Headline layer (`slide-1-headline`):** `[Concept Name].`
**Headline-accent layer (`slide-1-headline-accent`):** The concept name word(s) — set in DM Sans Bold, gold `#C8A96E`
**Body layer (`slide-1-body`):** Leave empty or add one clarifying line maximum.
**Label layer (`slide-1-label`):** `ATTUNE` (brand mark, Geist Mono, tracked caps, gold)
**Tone:** Still. Confident. The period is load-bearing.

**Copy prompt:** Write just the concept name as a complete sentence. One word or short phrase. Full stop. No explanation. Examples: "Attractor States." / "Affordance." / "Soft Assembly." / "The Constraints-Led Approach."

---

### Slide 2 — Tension
**Role:** Create cognitive dissonance. Most people think they know this. They don't.
**Headline layer (`slide-2-headline`):** `Here's what most people get wrong about it.`
**Body layer (`slide-2-body`):** 1-2 sentences naming the common misconception. Specific. Not vague.
**Tone:** Direct. Slightly provocative. Not condescending.

**Copy prompt:** What is the single most common misunderstanding of this concept? Name it precisely. Do not hedge. Example for "Attractor States": "Most coaches think attractor states are goals to aim at. They're not. They're basins the system falls into — whether you intend them or not."

---

### Slide 3 — Wrong View
**Role:** Steel-man the wrong view before demolishing it. Respect the mistake.
**Headline layer (`slide-3-headline`):** `The standard view:`
**Body layer (`slide-3-body`):** 2-3 sentences articulating the dominant (incorrect) understanding as it would be stated by someone who believes it. No straw-manning.
**Label layer (`slide-3-label`):** `THE PROBLEM` (Geist Mono, small, gold)
**Tone:** Fair. Precise. The reader should recognize their own thinking here.

**Copy prompt:** Write the wrong view from the inside — as its believers would state it. Make it sound reasonable. The reader should nod before the turn. Example: "Skill is learned through repetition. The more you repeat the correct movement, the more it becomes automatic. The coach's job is to demonstrate the right technique and correct deviations from it."

---

### Slide 4 — Right View
**Role:** The ecological reframe. The paradigm shift.
**Headline layer (`slide-4-headline`):** `The ecological view:`
**Body layer (`slide-4-body`):** 2-3 sentences stating the correct understanding. Named principle if applicable.
**Label layer (`slide-4-label`):** `THE SHIFT` (Geist Mono, small, gold)
**Accent layer (`slide-4-accent`):** Optional: one-line pull quote encapsulating the reframe. (Cormorant Garamond Italic, gold)
**Tone:** Assertive. Grounded in research but spoken in plain language.

**Copy prompt:** State the ecological view directly. Use Sam's core vocabulary where it fits naturally: affordance, attunement, coupling, emergence, constraints, attractor, perception-action. Do not over-explain. One conceptual move per slide.

---

### Slide 5 — Practice
**Role:** Ground the theory in a concrete, specific scenario. Sport first, then cross-domain.
**Headline layer (`slide-5-headline`):** `What this looks like in practice:`
**Body layer (`slide-5-body`):** 3-4 sentences. One specific scenario from coaching, sport, or skill development. Concrete enough to visualize.
**Tone:** Storytelling. Present tense. Scene-based.

**Copy prompt:** Put the concept in a training session or real-world moment. Name a sport, a drill, a decision. Example: "A discus thrower practicing full throws in a constrained space. The arc is shorter. The release angle shifts. The body finds a new solution — not because the coach prescribed it, but because the environment demanded it."

---

### Slide 6 — Mechanism
**Role:** Name the underlying principle precisely. This is the citation-worthy moment.
**Headline layer (`slide-6-headline`):** `The mechanism:`
**Body layer (`slide-6-body`):** 2-3 sentences. Name the principle, define it, cite a thinker if it strengthens the slide.
**Label layer (`slide-6-label`):** Thinker's name if cited (e.g., `GIBSON` / `BERNSTEIN` / `DAVIDS`) — Geist Mono, gold
**Tone:** Precise. Academic weight without academic language.

**Copy prompt:** Name the principle that explains WHY the ecological view is correct. Use: Bernstein's "repetition without repetition," Gibson's affordances, Newell's constraints model, Waddington's attractor landscape, Davids' ecological dynamics, Gray's variable goal training. Define it in one sentence a non-expert can understand.

---

### Slide 7 — Implication
**Role:** Cross-domain application. What does this mean for the reader, not just for athletes?
**Headline layer (`slide-7-headline`):** `Why this changes everything:`
**Body layer (`slide-7-body`):** 2-3 sentences applying the principle to a second domain: creation, parenting, business, writing, learning.
**Tone:** Expansive. The reader should feel the concept unlock something in their own domain.

**Copy prompt:** Cross the domain. Take the same concept and apply it to creation or coaching or parenting or business. Show the isomorphism without laboring it. Example: "A writer who only writes in one genre is training in a non-representative environment. The skill developed there doesn't transfer. The constraints of genre ARE the affordances. Vary them."

---

### Slide 8 — CTA
**Role:** Close with an action that compounds the relationship.
**Headline layer (`slide-8-headline`):** `Save this. Share it with someone who trains by prescription.`
**Body layer (`slide-8-body`):** Optional: 1-line teaser for Signal/Noise or Foundations. Example: "Signal/Noise goes deeper every week. Link in bio."
**Label layer (`slide-8-label`):** `@attunemastery` (Geist Mono, gold)
**Tone:** Direct. No summary. No "in conclusion." End in motion.

**Copy prompt:** Write the CTA as an invitation, not a command. The save request is load-bearing for the algorithm — keep it. Optionally add one line connecting to Signal/Noise or Foundations. Never summarize what was just covered.

---

## Layout Modules Used in EXPLAINER

**Slide 1:** Full-bleed headline. No body. Brand mark only. Maximum white space.
**Slides 2-4:** Headline + body text. Label on slide 3 and 4. Optional gold rule divider below headline.
**Slide 5:** Headline + body (scene-based storytelling). No label needed.
**Slide 6:** Headline + body + label (thinker name). This is the "credential slide."
**Slide 7:** Headline + body. Expansive, generous tone.
**Slide 8:** Headline only or headline + 1-line body. Label = @attunemastery. Maximum simplicity.

## Voice Checklist (run before finalizing all slides)

- [ ] First person (I) throughout — never "coaches should" or "one must"
- [ ] No em dashes — replace with colon or period
- [ ] No banned words: delve, realm, harness, unlock, tapestry, leverage, synergy, seamless, optimize
- [ ] No AI transitions: "Furthermore", "In conclusion", "It's worth noting", "Let's dive in"
- [ ] At least two domains crossed (sport + one other)
- [ ] Slide 1 starts INSIDE the concept — no thesis, no setup
- [ ] Each slide: one idea, stated completely, no cliff-hangers except slide 2
- [ ] Slide 8 ends with invitation or action — not summary
